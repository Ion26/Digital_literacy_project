# UPI Fraud Case Study

UPI (Unified Payments Interface) fraud is one of the most common types of cybercrime in India. In such frauds, attackers trick users into revealing sensitive information or approving malicious payment requests.

A typical scenario involves a fraudster calling or messaging the victim while pretending to be a bank representative or a buyer. The attacker sends a “collect request” on a UPI app and convinces the victim that accepting it will result in receiving money. In reality, accepting the request transfers money from the victim’s account to the fraudster.

Students and individuals who frequently use digital payment apps are common targets. Due to lack of awareness, many users unknowingly share OTPs or approve requests.

The consequences include financial loss, stress, and difficulty in recovering the stolen money. In some cases, victims lose large amounts within minutes.

This case highlights the importance of awareness and careful usage of digital payment platforms.
